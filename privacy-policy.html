Definitions GDPR Art. 4

Throughout this Privacy Policy, the following terms carry the meanings set out below unless the context requires otherwise. These definitions are intended to be consistent with applicable data protection legislation, including the GDPR, POPIA, CCPA, and equivalent statutes.

Personal Data / Personal Information
Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("data subject"), directly or indirectly, by reference to identifiers such as name, identification number, location data, online identifiers, or factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity of that natural person.
Processing
Any operation or set of operations performed on personal data, whether by automated means or not ‚Äî including collection, recording, organisation, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.
Data Controller / Responsible Party
ExamSidemann App Store, which alone or jointly with others determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data. In South Africa, referred to as the "Responsible Party" under POPIA.
Data Processor / Operator
A natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which processes personal data on behalf of the controller. In South Africa, referred to as the "Operator" under POPIA.
Data Subject
An identified or identifiable natural person whose personal data is processed. This includes you, as a user of our services.
Consent
Any freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous indication of the data subject's wishes, by a statement or a clear affirmative action, signifying agreement to the processing of personal data relating to them.
Supervisory Authority
An independent public authority established by a Member State or competent jurisdiction to supervise application of data protection law. Examples include the ICO (UK), CNIL (France), and the Information Regulator (South Africa).
Third Country
A country outside the European Economic Area (EEA), or a country not recognised as providing an adequate level of protection by the European Commission, from or to which personal data may be transferred.
Pseudonymisation
The processing of personal data in such a manner that it can no longer be attributed to a specific data subject without the use of additional information, where such additional information is kept separately and is subject to technical and organisational measures.
Anonymisation
The process of altering personal data so that it cannot be attributed to a specific individual, either directly or indirectly, even with the use of additional information.
Special Category Data / Sensitive Information
Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data processed for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, health data, or data concerning a person's sex life or sexual orientation.
Cookie
A small text file placed on a device by a website or app, used to identify the device, remember preferences, track usage patterns, and enable certain functionalities. Cookies may be first-party (set by us) or third-party (set by external services).
Services
All products, features, websites, mobile applications, APIs, and related services operated by ExamSidemann App Store, including but not limited to the web-based app store, review system, download manager, developer portal, and support channels.
Business Day
Any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in the Republic of South Africa or, where relevant to the applicable law, in the relevant jurisdiction.
Aggregate Data
Statistical or summarised information derived from personal data that does not identify any individual and cannot be used alone or in combination with other data to identify a specific person.
Profiling
Any form of automated processing of personal data consisting of the use of personal data to evaluate certain personal aspects relating to a natural person, in particular to analyse or predict aspects concerning that natural person's performance, behaviour, preferences, interests, reliability, location, or movements.

International Data Transfers GDPR Ch. V

As an online platform, we operate globally and our infrastructure is distributed across multiple countries. This means that personal data may be transferred to, stored in, and processed in countries other than your country of residence.

10.1 Transfers from the EEA / UK

Where we transfer personal data from the EEA or UK to countries that have not received an adequacy decision from the European Commission or UK Secretary of State, we implement appropriate safeguards, including:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): The EU Standard Contractual Clauses (2021/914) are incorporated into our data processing agreements with all relevant sub-processors in third countries.
  • UK International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTAs): Used for transfers from the UK following Brexit.
  • Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs): Where applicable for intra-group transfers within multinational processor organisations.
  • Adequacy Decisions: Where a country has received an adequacy decision (e.g., Japan, Canada, Argentina), we rely on that decision as the basis for the transfer.

10.2 Transfers from South Africa

Under POPIA Section 72, personal information may only be transferred to a foreign country if that country has equivalent protection or if the data subject consents, or if the transfer is necessary for the performance of a contract. We rely on contractual protections consistent with these requirements for all cross-border transfers from South Africa.

10.3 Transfer Impact Assessments

Where required by law or our internal data governance framework, we conduct Transfer Impact Assessments before initiating new categories of data transfers to third countries. These assessments evaluate the legal environment in the destination country and the technical and organisational measures in place to supplement any contractual safeguards.

10.4 Copies of Safeguards

A copy of the relevant safeguards we rely upon for international transfers may be obtained by contacting our Data Protection Officer at dpo@examsidemann.example.

Security Measures

We implement a comprehensive and layered security programme designed to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure, or access.

12.1 Technical Measures

  • Encryption in transit: All communications between your browser/app and our servers are encrypted using TLS 1.2 or TLS 1.3.
  • Encryption at rest: Stored personal data is encrypted using AES-256 or equivalent strong encryption in all production databases and object stores.
  • Password hashing: User passwords are stored as hashed values using bcrypt with an adaptive cost factor; we never store plaintext passwords.
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA): MFA is available and encouraged for all user accounts; it is mandatory for all administrative and developer accounts.
  • Access controls: Role-based access control (RBAC) ensures that employees and systems have access only to the data necessary for their specific functions (principle of least privilege).
  • Network segmentation: Our production environment is isolated from development and staging environments, and internal network traffic is segmented to limit the impact of any breach.
  • Web Application Firewall (WAF): Deployed at the network perimeter to detect and block common web attacks including SQL injection, XSS, and CSRF.
  • DDoS mitigation: Cloudflare and equivalent services provide continuous protection against distributed denial-of-service attacks.
  • Automated vulnerability scanning: Our infrastructure and codebase are subject to regular automated scans using industry-standard tools.
  • Penetration testing: We engage independent security professionals to conduct penetration tests at least annually.

12.2 Organisational Measures

  • All staff with access to personal data receive mandatory data protection and security training upon onboarding and annually thereafter.
  • Background checks are conducted on employees who have access to sensitive personal data.
  • Confidentiality agreements are signed by all employees, contractors, and third-party service providers.
  • A formal information security policy is maintained and reviewed annually.
  • An incident response plan is in place and tested regularly via tabletop exercises.
  • A data breach notification procedure ensures timely response and notification in the event of a breach (see Section 18).

12.3 Physical Measures

Our primary infrastructure is hosted in certified data centres (managed by Google Cloud) that maintain ISO 27001 certification, SOC 2 Type II attestation, and appropriate physical security controls including 24/7 surveillance, biometric access, and environmental monitoring.

Important: Despite our comprehensive security measures, no system is completely impenetrable. We encourage you to use a strong, unique password, to enable MFA, and to contact us immediately if you suspect any unauthorised activity on your account.

Data Breach Procedures & Notification

Despite our extensive security measures, no system is entirely immune to security incidents. In the event of a personal data breach, we have a robust incident response plan to minimise harm and ensure timely, transparent notification.

18.1 Detection & Containment

Upon becoming aware of a potential data breach, our security team will immediately:

  • Activate the incident response plan and assemble the incident response team
  • Contain the breach to prevent further unauthorised access or data loss
  • Preserve evidence for forensic investigation
  • Assess the nature, scope, and likely consequences of the breach

18.2 Regulatory Notification

Where a breach is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority:

  • Within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach (GDPR / UK GDPR requirement)
  • As soon as reasonably possible, or within 5 days, under POPIA
  • Within applicable timelines under CCPA, PIPEDA, and other relevant laws

18.3 Individual Notification

Where a breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, we will notify affected users without undue delay. Notification will include:

  • A description of the nature of the breach
  • The likely consequences of the breach
  • The categories and approximate number of records affected
  • The measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its possible adverse effects
  • Contact details of our Data Protection Officer for further information
  • Recommended steps for affected individuals to protect themselves (e.g., changing passwords, monitoring account activity)

18.4 Remediation & Post-Incident Review

Following any breach, we will conduct a root cause analysis, implement remediation measures, and review our security controls to prevent recurrence. Lessons learned are incorporated into our security programme.

Anonymisation & Pseudonymisation

We apply pseudonymisation and anonymisation techniques wherever appropriate to reduce privacy risks while enabling continued use of data for analytical and operational purposes.

26.2 Pseudonymisation

Where data needs to be retained in a form that can be re-linked to an individual (for example, to allow you to request deletion of your data), we apply pseudonymisation by replacing direct identifiers with tokens or hashes. Pseudonymised data remains personal data and is subject to all applicable data protection requirements; the re-identification key is stored separately with access strictly controlled.

26.3 K-Anonymity & Differential Privacy

For certain analytics outputs and developer-facing statistics, we apply k-anonymity principles to ensure that no individual can be identified within a published dataset. We are also exploring the application of differential privacy techniques to further enhance privacy protections in aggregate reporting.
